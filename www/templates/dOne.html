When indicating neoplasms as a cause of death, include the following:

  • the primary site or that the primary site is unknown,
  • whether benign or malignant, and
  • any secondary sites of metastases, if applicable.

If known, also indicate the cell type, grade and/or stage, and the part or lobe of the organ affected. For example, a well-defined squamous cell carcinoma of the left lung, upper lobe. To see an example, please click here.
